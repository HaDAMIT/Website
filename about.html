About

The vast expanse of our oceans, encompassing more than 70% of the Earth's surface, holds immeasurable importance for sustaining life on our planet.

Human actions such as overfishing, pollution, climate change, coastal development and plastic pollution have caused extensive damage to the oceans.

Goal 14 aims to promote the conservation and sustainable use of marine ecosystems and resources to ensure their future viability.

Plastic Pollution

Plastic pollution in oceans is a global crisis, endangering marine life and ecosystems. Billions of tons of plastic waste harmlessly discarded end up in the oceans each year, creating severe environmental and ecological challenges.

Targets

Target 14.1

By 2025, prevent and significantly reduce marine pollution of all kinds, in particular from land-based activities, including marine debris and nutrient pollution. The global trend of elevated coastal eutrophication continued in 2022 above the 2000-2004 baseline conditions, though different in magnitude from recent years. The highest rates are in the Arabian Sea.

Target 14.3

The United Nations aim to minimize and address the impacts of ocean acidification, including through enhanced scientific cooperation at all levels. Ocean acidification is increasing and will continue to do so if CO2 emissions do not stop rising, threatening marine ecosystems and the services they provide. Today, the ocean's average pH is 8.1 which means that the ocean today is about 30% more acidic than in pre-industrial times.
