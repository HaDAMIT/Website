UN Goal 14: Life Below Water

The goal Life Below Water aims to ensure the sustainable use and conservation of oceans, seas, and marine resources to protect marine ecosystems and support livelihoods.

Learn More







Overfishing

Unsustainable fishing practices devastate marine ecosystems and deplete global fish populations at alarming rates.

Marine Pollution

Plastics, chemicals, oil spills, and wastewater harm marine ecosystems, with 8 million tonnes of plastic waste entering the oceans annually.

Habitat Destruction

Coastal development, destructive fishing practices, and climate change degrade and destroy vital marine habitats, including coral reefs.

Climate Change

Rapid climate change poses a grave threat to the environment, impacting oceans and exacerbating the challenges of overfishing.

Coral Bleaching

Rising ocean temperatures and climate change cause widespread coral bleaching, endangering reefs and marine biodiversity.
