Modern Innovative Technologies/Strategies

Addressing the 'Life Below Water' goal requires the implementation of modern innovative technologies and strategies. These can be achieved at both a global level, and within Normanhurst Boys High School. Some examples are listed below:

Ocean-based renewable energy

Technology

Ocean-based renewable energy, such as wave, tidal, and offshore wind power, has the potential to provide clean, renewable energy sources for people around the world.

Ocean Cleanup Systems

Technology

These systems involve the deployment of floating barriers and screens that use natural ocean currents to concentrate and collect plastic debris. The collected debris can then be properly managed and recycled.

Satellite Monitoring

Technology

Satellite-based technologies enable the monitoring of fishing activities and the identification of illegal, unreported, and unregulated (IUU) fishing. Remote sensing data can track vessels, identify fishing hotspots, and support enforcement efforts.

Conservation of marine resources

Strategy

This includes protecting and restoring coastal and marine ecosystems, reducing pollution and overfishing, and promoting sustainable fishing practices. By implementing these initiatives, we can help conserve our marine resources and ensure the continued health of our oceans.

Sustainable aquaculture

Strategy

Aquaculture, or the farming of fish and other aquatic animals, can be done sustainably by using responsible management practices, including the use of sustainable feed sources, minimizing environmental impacts, and properly managing waste.

Marine protected areas

Strategy

Marine protected areas, or MPAs, are areas where human activities are limited or prohibited in order to protect the biodiversity and habitats of the marine environment.
