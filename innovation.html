Modern Innovative Technologies/Strategies

Addressing the 'Life Below Water' goal requires the implementation of modern innovative technologies and strategies. These can be achieved at both a global level, and within Normanhurst Boys High School. Some examples are listed below:

Ocean-based renewable energy

Technology

Ocean-based renewable energy, such as wave, tidal, and offshore wind power, is a way of providing clean, renewable energy sources to people around the world.

Ocean Cleanup Systems

Technology

These systems involve the deployment of floating barriers and screens that use natural ocean currents to concentrate and collect plastic debris. The collected debris can then be properly managed and recycled.

Satellite Monitoring

Technology

Satellite-based technologies enable the monitoring of fishing activities and the identification of illegal, unreported, and unregulated (IUU) fishing. Remote sensing data can track vessels, identify fishing hotspots, and support enforcement efforts.

Conservation of marine resources

Strategy

Conservation of marine resources aims to protect and restore coastal and marine ecosystems by reducing pollution, overfishing and promoting sustainable fishing practices.

Sustainable aquaculture

Strategy

Aquaculture is the growing of commercial marine products.. Through the use of responsible management practices such as the use of sustainable feed and minimising environmental impacts can make aqauculture a sustainable practice.

Marine protected areas

Strategy

We can reinforce marine protected areas. Marine protected areas are where human activities are prohibited in order to protect the marine environment.

Online Citizen Science

Technology

Engage students in online citizen science projects focused on marine research and conservation. Platforms like Zooniverse or iNaturalist allow students to contribute to real scientific data collection efforts.

Sustainability Monitoring Systems

Technology

Implement monitoring systems within the school premises to track energy consumption, water usage, and waste generation. Displaying real-time data in common areas can raise awareness.

Virtual Reality (VR)

Technology

Use VR technology to simulate underwater ecosystems, allowing students to explore and understand marine environments firsthand.

Education and Awareness

Strategy

Promote education and awareness about SDG 14 among students, staff, and the wider community. Organize workshops, seminars, and awareness campaigns to inform students about the importance of marine conservation.

Sustainable Fisheries Awareness

Strategy

Organize programs and workshops to educate students about sustainable fishing practices. Involve local fishing communities, marine biologists, or government organizations to provide insights into sustainable fishing techniques, responsible consumption, and the importance of protecting marine ecosystems.

Collaboration with Marine Conservation Organizations

Strategy

Establish partnerships with local marine conservation organizations to engage students in practical conservation initiatives. This can include participating in beach cleanups, coral reef restoration projects, or adopting nearby coastal areas to monitor and protect marine biodiversity.

  • Donations

    Donations can fund multiple technologies and strategies. Even the smallest donation can make a difference to our oceans. The easiest way to donate is through the UN Website.

  • Recycling

    By actively participating in recycling programs and properly sorting recyclable materials, we can reduce waste, and prevent them from polluting the oceans.

  • Buy Local and Certified Fish

    Supporting small-scale fish producers by purchasing locally sourced and certified fish helps promote sustainable fishing practices, ensuring the long-term health of marine ecosystems

  • Raise Awareness

    By raising awareness about the harmful consequences of pollution through educational campaigns, we can encourage individuals to make sustainable choices.

  • Stay Informed

    By staying updated on local news and connecting with organisations on social media, we can stay informed about environmental issues relating to our oceans
